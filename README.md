## 📊 Key Insights

* Most passengers were between **20–40 years** of age.
* Third-class passengers represented the largest group.
* Female passengers had a significantly higher survival rate than male passengers.
* First-class passengers were more likely to survive than passengers in lower classes.
* Ticket fares were positively associated with survival.
* The fare distribution was highly right-skewed with several high-value outliers.
* Southampton was the most common embarkation port.
* Passenger class strongly influenced both fare and survival.
* Correlation analysis showed only a few strong relationships among numerical variables.
* Data visualization helped uncover hidden patterns and trends within the dataset.
